Publications

Advancing Neural Architecture Search with Efficient Sampling Strategies

Bryce Wang, Jane Smith, Dr. Michael Johnson

International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML) 2024

We propose a novel sampling strategy for neural architecture search that reduces computational overhead by 60% while maintaining competitive performance. Our approach leverages evolutionary algorithms combined with gradient-based optimization to efficiently explore the architecture space.

Multimodal Learning for Enhanced Human-AI Collaboration

Bryce Wang, Dr. Sarah Chen, Prof. David Lee

Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI) 2024

This work explores how multimodal AI systems can better understand human intent through combined visual, textual, and gestural inputs. We demonstrate significant improvements in task completion rates and user satisfaction in collaborative scenarios.

Interpretable Deep Learning for Medical Image Analysis

Dr. Emily Rodriguez, Bryce Wang, Prof. Robert Kim

Nature Machine Intelligence 2023

We develop an interpretable deep learning framework for medical image analysis that provides both accurate predictions and clinically meaningful explanations. The method achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple medical imaging benchmarks.

Federated Learning with Privacy-Preserving Mechanisms

Bryce Wang, Dr. Lisa Zhang, Prof. Mark Thompson

IEEE Transactions on Information Forensics and Security 2023

We present a federated learning framework that incorporates differential privacy and secure aggregation to protect user data while maintaining model performance. Our approach shows promising results on large-scale distributed datasets.

Research Interests

My research is driven by the vision of creating AI systems that are not only powerful but also trustworthy, interpretable, and beneficial to society. I'm particularly interested in the intersection of machine learning and human-computer interaction, where technology meets human needs.

Currently, I'm exploring how large language models can be made more efficient and reliable, with a focus on developing methods for better human-AI collaboration. I believe that the future of AI lies in systems that can work seamlessly with humans while being transparent about their capabilities and limitations.

I'm always open to collaborations and discussions about research opportunities. If you're interested in working together or have ideas to share, please don't hesitate to reach out!
